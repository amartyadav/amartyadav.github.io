Projects

The Blind Stick

This was my first project with Arduino. An upgrade to an usual walking stick, the Blind Stick has two ultrasonic emitters and receivers, one on the front and one at the bottom of the stick, enclosed securely. On the principle of SONAR, the sensors could immediately calculate the distance between the person carrying the stick and any obstacle in the front and/or below. If the distance between the two crossed the safe threshold, say 50 cm, a buzzer would fire up. And say if the person is in a loud area, then to make up for the buzzer alarm, a vibrating motor would strongly vibrate the stick, and thus warning the person of the obstacle.



Time Lock

The time-based lock was meant to ease the usual locking-unlocking hassle of labs in schools and universities. To set it up, all a user has to do is fill in the timetable of lab usage in the software, that was especially developed for the lock, called TBLTable. The software then uploaded the timetable to the cloud. The lock’s microcontroller was smart enough to read the timetable from the cloud(not technically speaking) and open the lock at designated times as in the timetable entered by the user. This reduced the hassle of manually unlocking the door by every student and then locking it back again after the lab’s use was done.
